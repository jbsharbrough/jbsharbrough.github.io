eBusiness:  What is it anyway? 

The old paradigm: EDP, MIS and DSS still live.

There’s nothing new under the sun.

In the early 80s, the old days of departmental computing, programmers were taught that there were three kinds of computer applications:

Electronic Data Processing (EDP). This generally referred to the automation of a task which was previously performed manually. The printing of checks, statements and dunning letters are examples of early EDP applications.
Management Information Systems (MIS). This type of application gathered information about the internal organizational performance for reporting to managers. The preparation of monthly sales reports; weekly comparative sales reports by salesman, region, and product; production summaries and manpower reports were all MIS applications.
Decision Support Systems (DSS). The higher a manager climbed an organization chart, the more external information was required. DSS were databases that gathered internal and external data for decisions that weren’t bases solely on internal information.

The marriage of digital networks and computing produced a number of opportunities to achieve old tasks in new ways that are faster and more cost effective. New opportunities to create internal databases for management summary abound. Databases of external information such as competitor prices can be built using simple utilities.
