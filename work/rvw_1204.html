Discoveries, June 23, 1997

by Beau Sharbrough
The Discoveries section of the Dallas Morning News often features articles of relevance to non-linear dynamic systems.  The various sections are here identified, and where there is special interest, reviewed.

NEUROSCIENCE

DOORS TO PERCEPTION: Scientists look to vision research as avenue to understanding consciousness

The brain manipulates sensory input to create mental pictures, or "percepts."

The function of awareness is to take what's currently around me, make a compact representation, filtered through my experience, my memories, my expectations, and make this accessible to planning," according to Dr Christof Koch at CalTech Pasadena.

Blindsight -- being able to perform a task that you can't describe or see -- tells researchers about how the brain works. Researchers assume that the perception does not make it to the "conscious" brain. I think that the jury is out on that. It doesn't make it to the verbal brain. It may be that the conscious brain is not one and the same thing as the place where we think words.

Researchers say that philosophers have tried to explain consciousness for 2,000 years without success. They want a scientific explanation. It sounds like a mechanistic explanation, and I'll be happy to watch them talk about it on Oprah. Oh, darn. I don't watch Oprah -- I'm at work!

ASTRONOMY

Space telescope's makeover yields sharper insights into the universe.

The February 1997 makeover of the Hubble has produced "a new window into the early universe." While one of the new cameras isn't working at all, the infrared images are helping scientists explore.

SIEGFRIED

Science's high-tech distortions magnify economic inequality

Freeman Dyson says that scientific advances have created a larger wealth gap. Machines have displaced unskilled labor. In the 20s, JBS Haldane said that it was because science has no "ethical guidance. And society has no ethical context for coping with the technological gifts that science confers."

Mr Dyson is sensitive to the River Jordan that has always existed between the haves and the have nots. You can cross it, but very few people go both ways

I think that it might be that the haves are able to act on opportunities in ways that the wannabes cannot. Science produces many new opportunities. Just wait until Bill Gates gives away a billion dollars. Someone else, perhaps less fortunate but also tuned to opportunities, will cross that river between poverty and porsche.
