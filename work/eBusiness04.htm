eBusiness:  What is it anyway?

The new understanding:
Systems analysis of costs and benefits.

We are the betting animal.

Deciding what new data to gather and what to do with it is not a simple choice. Cause and effect are very hard to tease out sometimes. Peter Senge offers hope in his book The Fifth Discipline. This book identifies and explains several of the basic cause and effect relationships that compose systems analysis. The ability to analyze a system and understand cause and effect is a critical skill when being the first to discover the cause and effect relationship is so critical.

Until the Internet came along, knowing how a system worked was important, but being able to act on that understanding was sometimes quite difficult. Getting audience with your customers to explain a new service offering could take a business several months of face to face meetings by the sales force, for example. Now a business can put a new service offering on its website, email the information instantly to its customers, suppliers and employees, and voila! The new program is rolled out.

The relationship between the value of understanding and the value of communicating just changed. Communication is much simpler, and understanding is just as hard as ever.
