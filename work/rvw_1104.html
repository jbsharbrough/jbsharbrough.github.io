This article opens with one of the wisest paragraphs I've seen:

A disorganized life is a series of missed opportunities. Lesser concerns wind up stealing our time and energy, while people and activities we care about suffer for lack of attention.

Lessons in Traveling Smarter and Cheaper

These people went around the world with five children and an infant grandchild in 34 days for $29,700. I can already tell that they are brilliant organizers and managers because who can get their kids together for a month? Included in the article are ideas for:

Saving five percent on air fares AFTER you find the best deal all by yourself.
Planning for splitting the group on the plane.
Finding affordable lodging.
Cutting back on luggage.
What to be sure you DO carry. This list includes binoculars.

The Bottom Line is great: "Don't wait for the perfect opportunity for a dream trip. Perfect opportunities never arise." You can get some great ones if you're willing to do the best you can.
