PUSH Scene 04

SCENE 4: ROGER and Ellen, at home again. they are playing scrabble. Or at least they are just starting.

They each turn up a letter.

ELLEN

E.

ROGER

I go first. Let’s see here. Ik, STAR.

ELLEN

Very funny. LAUGH.

ROGER

SONATA. You don’t have a chance, you know.

ELLEN

I know. You know everything. I’ve never won yet, why should I start? QUIT.

ROGER

HIT. I’m a poet. My feet show it. They’re longfellows.

 

ELLEN

I never knew that. You’re very QUICK.

 

ROGER

You’re very funny. You ought to be on the STAGE. One leaves at ten tonight.

 

ELLEN

I think you’re off your ROCKER.

 

ROGER

Cut this childish punishment and behave like and ADULT.

 

ELLEN

ACQUIT. It’ll never happen to you.

 

ROGER

Two words for me. LOST and OR.

 

ELLEN

I can’t stand your sense of HUMOR.

 

ROGER

You’re in a drought yourself. Bad CROP this year.

 

ELLEN

I can’t HANDLE this.

 

ROGER

Ready for this? I’ll bury you now. MICROPHONE.

 

ELLEN

You turd! I was gonna go there. Ok, ADULTERY.

 

ROGER

CELEB.

 

ELLEN

That’s no word!

 

ROGER

I just said it, didn’t I?

 

ELLEN

I can say glixmix, can’t I?

 

ROGER

It’s short for celebrity. Rona Barrett even says it.

 

ELLEN

Well, I guess it’s impeccable, then. But don’t let Edwin Newman hear you.

COY.

 

ROGER

I gotta get some big points. DANCE.

 

ELLEN

LAY.

 

ROGER

BLONDE.

 

ELLEN

EMPTY.

 

ROGER

SAX.

 

ELLEN

SEX blank. SEXY.

 

ROGER

SING.

 

ELLEN

TOUCH. I’m out. What’ve you got?

 

ROGER

Ellen, there’s something strange about all of the words you used.

 

ELLEN

They’re all legal. I win. Haha, I beat you, I never beat you. Let’s get drunk.

 

ROGER

No, I mean that they have a certain theme to them.

 

ELLEN

What’s that?

 

ROGER

Look at them. Coy, lay, touch, adultery, quit, empty, sexy … What are you trying to say?

 

ELLEN

Pardon me, Herr Freud, but I’m trying to say, "I win."

 

ROGER

Well, what do you wanna do now, watch TV?

 

ELLEN

No, I don’t wanna watch TV. We always watch TV. I’m sick of TV. Let’s do something different. Let’s go somewhere.

 

ROGER

I don’t wanna go out.

 

ELLEN

I know, you don’t feel like being seen, right?

 

ROGER

You don’t have to be so thoughtless and mean, Ellen.

 

ELLEN

Sorry, roger. Just joking.

 

ROGER

And were those dirty words in the game a joke, too?

 

ELLEN

I don’t pick the letter, Roger. I just play them.

 

ROGER

The story of my life.

 

ELLEN

I see what you’re saying, Roger, but I don’t have anything to say about it.

 

ROGER

I do. I want to have a home, and to feel like everything here is mine, and something I made out of nothing, something that would never have been if not for me. This house is my mark on the world, and the feeling between us just isn’t the glue I need to build the rest of my life. Do you still like me? Do you hurry home from work to see me?

 

ELLEN

You know I do. I’m always here by six and we eat by seven.

 

ROGER

I mean something else. You have a career, and you get out in the public, and you mix with other people, but I don’t. I stay at home all day and never go anywhere and never do anything and I’m different. Do you hear? I’m different! I’m not like you and I’m not like your friends and I don’t do what you do and these days we don’t agree about anything!

 

ELLEN

Roger, I don’t

 

ROGER

I’m sick of this! I’m sick of Beeler and I’m sick of arguing and I’m sick of not doing anything and I’m sick. Take care of me, Ellen. Make me feel good again. Just like before. Make it like before.

 

ELLEN

It can’t ever be like before. It’s always changing. And I don’t want to change it. I like it like it is now, with a little change and a little challenge and a little variety, and I’m not sick of my life.

I’m just sick of you. You and your whining. You act so tough six days a week and you break down on the seventh. (SHE STARTS CRYING) I can’t take the way you never act affectionate when I want it, and then cry when you want it. I can’t take the way you gripe about me bringing my friends over. I can’t take you saying I’m dumb. I’m not dumb and I can hope that life would hold a chance for me to be normal and not have to be dumb. I don’t wan … I … you’re so different, all right …

 

ROGER

It won’t work, Ellen. It won’t work. I need your help. I need you.

 

ELLEN

I can’t help you, Roger, because you can’t help yourself. Why don’t you get your act together and then give me a call.

SHE GOES OUT TO THE BEDROOM AND COMES BACK WITH A BAG.

ROGER

Where are you … ?

 

ELLEN

Just why don’t you get your act together and give me a call?

SHE GOES OUT.

ROGER

(TO HIMSELF) Ok. Ok. That’s just what I’ll do. I’ll just get my act together.

 

FADEOUT
