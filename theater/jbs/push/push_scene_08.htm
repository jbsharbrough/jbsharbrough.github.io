PUSH Scene 08

SCENE 8: Ellen and Larry in his apartment. He is watching TV. He is mumbling while he reads and she is getting increasingly perturbed about it. Right when she starts to say something about it, he stops, and reads silently, seeming totally unaware of the crisis he has so narrowly averted. This happens several times.

 

ELLEN

Geez, Larry, cut it out.

 

LARRY

Huh?

 

ELLEN

Cut it out?

 

LARRY

Cut what out?

 

ELLEN

Right. You know what I mean. Cut it out.

 

LARRY

I can’t cut it out if I don’t know what it is.

 

ELLEN

That mumbling. You mumble when you read. You read all the time. You mumble all the time, right. And just when I start to say something about it, you stop for a minute, just long enough for me to get interested in the TV, and then you start it up again. Cut it out.

(LARRY GOES BACK TO HIS BOOK, CHOOSING NOT TO ANSWER)


Larry, do you think this place is big enough for the two of us? I mean, big enough for two people? I’ve been meaning to ask, I just don’t have room for my things in the closet, or the bathroom, and your refrigerator …

 

LARRY

No, this place is plenty big enough. I moved here because I like it and I still like it and I hate moving Can’t we just make do?

 

ELLEN

I’ve always made do and I’m sick of making do. Let’s do this right.

 

LARRY

Right? We are doing it right. You’re a guest in my house and my way is the right way.

 

ELLEN

I don’t believe I’m hearing this.

 

LARRY

Hearing what?

 

ELLEN

Hearing all of this disagreement. I had the impression that we were very compatible. I love you and I can’t believe that we’re fighting.

 

LARRY

Well, we are, and we’re not finished. Let’s go to the store.

 

ELLEN

Now? It’s 9:30 and the stores are all closed.

 

LARRY

The grocery store, lady. The grocery store. I want some munchies.

 

ELLEN

Let’s just munch right here.

SHE BEGINS TO KISS HIM, FIRST ON THE FACE AND NECK, THEN THEY ARE EMBRACING AND BOTH ARE KISSING, AND SUDDENLY LARRY ROLLS AWAY FROM HER.

ELLEN

What are you doing over there? Come here …

 

LARRY

I like it over here.

 

ELLEN

You’ve done that three times in a row now. You used to hold me and love me.

 

LARRY

I don’t feel like it. I don’t need to hold you right now. I’ll hold you later. (GETTING UP) Let’s get dressed. I still need to go to the store.

 

ELLEN

You’re not kidding. You’re really not kidding. (SHE GETS UP AND STARTS TO GO, AND THEN STOPS, AND SAYS) Well, you can just go by yourself. I’m gonna stay here and watch TV. I can’t figure it out. (SHE STARTS TO CRY) I thought I knew you, that we were just alike, you said we were two peas in a pod, and now I don’t think we’re really alike at all.

 

LARRY

I haven’t changed. I haven’t done anything different than I’ve always done. We are alike, some, but that doesn’t mean that we’re alike about everything. I’m going to the store alone, then, eh? I’ll be back later.

HE GOES OUT.

ELLEN

Rumplestiltskin … I don’t believe it. I could have … I was just sure that I knew what I’m doing … Now? Now now now … I just don’t don’t don’t know. I don’t know this guy at all. I’m living with a total stranger. He may like to hit women. He may be a little gay on the side. He may even be Catholic. Oh, Christ, what will I do with myself now?

THE TV IS ON THE TONIGHT SHOW. THE HOST CAN BE ANYONE.

HOST

America, we’d like to welcome a terrific new talent. Please welcome the song and dance talents of ROGER FALTRY!!!

ELLEN SNAPS TO, AND ROGER COMES OUT IN A TUX, WEARING A TOP HAT AND GLOVES AND A MASK OR MIME FACE. HE SINGS "LAY IT DOWN."
