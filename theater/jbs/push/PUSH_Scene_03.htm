SCENE 3: A modest apartment. the gorilla from shipping lives here. he is really no gorilla, but a very pleasant young man, as a rule, except when he’s confused, which isn’t very often.

He is reading, and as he reads, he mumbles the words to himself. The scene opens with Larry, sitting in a rocker, mumbling to himself. His Stereo is on. There is a knock at the door, and the gorilla gets up, puts down his newspaper, turns down the stereo, and answers the door.

Larry

Who is it?

ELLEN

It’s me.

 

LARRY

Who’s "me?"

 

ELLEN

"Who am I."

 

LARRY

That’s what I said!

 

ELLEN

Well how should I know? You haven’t even opened the door!

 

LARRY

(OPENING THE DOOR.) What’s going on here?

 

ELLEN

Hi, I’m Ellen Faltry. I’m taking a poll for Old America. Do you mind if I ask you a few questions?

 

LARRY

You sound more like a detective. Sure, go ahead.

 

ELLEN

This is a four-part questionnaire, with two questions for validating the other four. Question 1…

 

LARRY

Wait a minute. How long does this take?

 

ELLEN

Not long. Only a few moments of your time, Mr…

 

LARRY

Do I have to give my name for these questions? Are you trying to scare up some communists or something?

 

ELLEN

No, you don’t have to give you name. I only asked for the sake of politeness. I was just, you know…

 

LARRY

Well, in that case, I’m Larry Byers. And they’re everywhere, you know.

 

ELLEN

The Byers?

 

LARRY

The communists. They’re everywhere. They tried to take of Atlanta last week. Won’t you come in and sit down, Miss…

 

ELLEN

Faltry. Mrs.

 

LARRY

Mrs. Oh. Well, you’ll find it easier to write or something if you come in and sit down. That’s it. Make yourself comfortable, Mrs Faltry, and just…

 

ELLEN

Oh, what a nice, where did you get these rockers?

 

LARRY

My grandparents carried them out to Oklahoma in the 20’s. I refinished them when they passed down.

 

ELLEN

Oh, they’re so nice.

 

LARRY

Here, sit in this one, Mrs Faltry. Do you need a pillow? No, of course not. Just, just make yourself, no, of course.

 

ELLEN

Ellen.

 

LARRY

What?

 

ELLEN

Ellen. Call me Elle, Mr Byers.

 

LARRY

Call me Larry, Ellen.

 

ELLEN

Ok, Larry. Question 1: The president has been advised that we are being inundated by a mass immigration of illegal aliens. May of them are taking jobs here in the US, as sub-standard wages. Some people believe that the president should order a mass roundup of these aliens and deport them. Are you in favor of this policy?

 

LARRY

Which aliens? You mean Mexicans?

 

ELLEN

Well, not only Mexican nationals, but Greeks, Larry, Poles, Vietnamese, English professionals…

 

LARRY

I think we oughta keep the Mexicans. I love Mexican food. Do you like tacos? Not the hard ones, but the real tacos, the ones in the soft flour tortillas?

 

ELLEN

Yes, I do. I have a recipe from my aunt in El Paso/ (PAUSE) Then you are opposed to the policy of deporting all of the illegal aliens, Larry?

 

LARRY

Only the ones making over $2 per hour. The rest are probably doing us a favor. Do you like hamburgers?

 

ELLEN

Larry, this will take a week if you ask the questions. Yes I like hamburgers. The second question. The Congress has recently raised the minimum wage and the social security tax. Do you think that these moves will help our country find a stable economic base?

 

LARRY

No.

 

ELLEN

Why not?

 

LARRY

The wage increase is blatantly inflationary and the tax I pas the SSA will never come back to me. They’ll use my tax to keep some old geezer alive who already made more money than I’ll ever see, and they won’t save it for me, when I need it. Besides, I’m paying them in dollars worth a dollar. They’ll pay me back in dollars worth a quarter.

 

ELLEN

Do you think the Congress acted in bad faith by passing these bills?

 

LARRY

How do I know? They don’t tell me why they pass bills. They don’t tell me anything. I don’t have the faintest idea why they do what they do. Do you?

 

ELLEN

Do I what?

 

LARRY

Do you know why they do what they do?

 

ELLEN

Sure, they do it because their wives tell them to.

LAUGHTER REIGNS SUPREME. BOTH HAVE CRACKED UP, AND LARRY IS ROLLING ON THE FLOOR. WHEN THE LAUGHTER PASSES, HE HAS TEARS IN HIS EYES.

LARRY

I can’t believe that.

 

ELLEN

It’s true.

 

LARRY

So you like hamburgers, then. I'm fixing to eat now. Will you have a bite while we finish these questions?

 

ELLEN

Well, I really need to see thirteen more people tonight.

 

LARRY

Great! We can make up some good names and some really radical answers. Sound like fun?

ELLEN THINKS FOR A MINUTE, AND DECIDES TO PLAY

ELLEN

Sure, Larry. Sounds great.

 

LARRY

Ask me some more questions. It’s not often I get a married lady to come to dinner. You just ask the questions and I’ll get the dinner ready.

During the next few moments, Larry comes in and goes out to the kitchen, and the conversation is unbroken, with him talking form the kitchen when necessary.

ELLEN

Let me help you.

 

LARRY

Oh nononononono … I won’t hear of it. You’re my guest, and you’re gonna get the first class treatment. Ask away.

 

ELLEN

Maybe I should be going. I shouldn’t be imposing on you like this.

 

LARRY

Let me tell you something. Once there was this king, see, and he was a great king and all of his people, his, uh, his subjects, all of them loved him. He wasn’t married, this king wasn’t married, and all of his subjects wanted him to be happy, and they thought he would be happy if he was married, maybe you know something about this, and they were always sending women to his house and trying, you know, trying to make him really happy, you see, and he had so many women, so many around that he almost got tired of seeing so many women. I’m not a king, and nobody but Old America sends women to my house, and I like it so much I’m going to fix you a nice bug juicy burger.

 

ELLEN

Gee Larry, I like stories. We should do this more … Oh, I really must go. I’m sorry to have barged in on you like this. Thank you, you’re so nice, I mean…

AND SHE RUSHES OUT OF HIS APARTMENT.

LARRY

And I never even got to answer the other questions. No wonder things are so silly these days.

HE SITS DOWN AND, WITH A HAMBURGER IN EACH HAND, TAKES A BITE OUT OF EACH ONE, ALTERNATELY. HE MAKES A FACE THAT SAYS IT’S NOT SO BAD, AFTER ALL.
