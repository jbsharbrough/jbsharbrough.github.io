PUSH Scene 06

Scene 6: Larry Byers, alone in his apartment. He is reading the papers. He is mumbling to himself as he reads. The scene starts out slowly. Ellen knocks on his door.

 

LARRY

Uh, hello.

 

ELLEN

Hi.

 

LARRY

(LONG PAUSE. VERY LONG.) Hello.

 

ELLEN

I still have a few more questions.

 

LARRY

(LAUGHS NERVOUSLY) Come in, come in. I was just reading. (ELLEN STEPS INSIDE THE DOOR. HE SEES THE SUITCASE.) Those must be really long questions.

 

ELLEN

Number 1, do you like married women? And number 2, do you have room for my toothbrush?

 

LARRY

Yeah, uh, yeah, but … well, hell, Ellen, what’s going on?

 

ELLEN

I don’t have anywhere to go. And there’s no place I could go without coming here.

 

LARRY

I’ve given that a lot of thought. Don’t I get an explanation or something?

 

ELLEN

I ask all the questions. Do you think touching is better than talking?

 

LARRY

Yes.

 

ELLEN

What’s your favorite color?

 

LARRY

Blue.

 

ELLEN

Did you like the cheerleaders in high school?

 

LARRY

Yeah, but they wouldn’t go out with me.

 

e

Good, here’s your chance.

 

LARRY

You’re a cheerleader?

 

ELLEN

Class of ’70, Turner Warriors.

 

LARRY

I went to Turner. I don’t remember you.

 

ELLEN

That’s because the cheerleaders wouldn’t go out with you.

 

LARRY

Of course. I should have known. So, you’re just in time for the best tacos in town. I was getting ready to get up and go fix them when you knocked.

 

ELLEN

Larry?

 

LARRY

Yo.

 

ELLEN

Do you have a girlfriend or something?

 

LARRY

Sorry, you’re looking at the original Big Green Frog. The princess hasn’t kissed me yet. Tough life.

 

ELLEN

That’s good. I mean, that’s a shame. I mean, I’m glad.

She comes in all the way, and we see a film, of Larry and Ellen, riding in a car, laughing, at work, smiling at each other. At the supermarket, she points at a can of soup, he nods, she laughs. At his apartment, a fire in the fireplace, laughing together and in each other’s arms.
