SCENE 1: THE FALTRY LIVING ROOM

The TV is on, and the Faltrys have just finished breakfast. He is smoking. It is cold outside, cold and windy.

ELLEN

I just wish you weren’t so damned picky about your food. It’s all I can do to wake up before noon and you’re complaining before my heart even starts beating! It’s not even nine o’clock and I’ve already got a headache.

I get a lot of headaches lately.

ROGER

Um…

ELLEN

It just ruins my whole day…

ROGER

Um…

ELLEN

I can’t think straight and I’m afraid I’m going to wreck the car and THERE YOU GO putting ashes on the floor again! Don’t think that just because you’re invisible I can’t see you’re a slob! I’m not gonna clean up after you any more if you can’t care any more about our house, it’s our house, Roger, if you don’t care more about our house than to drop ashes all over the floor and then leave them for me to clean up after you!

 

ROGER

Um…

ELLEN

What, you found a sale in the paper?

ROGER

No, but I did find something interesting. They’re gonna make Arnold Rollieman’s new novel into a film for television.

ELLEN

So what? Haven’t you been listening to me?

ROGER

I think that’s such a shame, they’ll just mess it all up, just like his last one, and all those commercials, and no sensitivity, it just shouldn’t happen to such a nice guy…

ELLEN

That’s not what you said when you saw him at that party and he wouldn’t talk to you – you said he was the biggest snob in the world. You said you’d never talk to another writer as long as you lived. Are you listening to me?

ROGER

Um…

ELLEN

Does that mean yes? Or no?

ROGER

Isn’t it about time for you to go to work or something?

 

ELLEN

Sure, yes, it is, but couldn’t I call in sick? It’s been so long since we spent a day together, just us, and I kinda wish, well … No, I guess I oughta go … But we just don’t seem close anymore, we don’t go out together, we don’t stay home together, at least I think we don’t, and it’s just … it’s just not like it used to be.

(SHE GOES TO THE SINK)

Just look at those dishes, I can’t stay caught up, can I? Oh, there’s Celebrity Sweepstakes. See if you can win, Roger. I know you like it, even though I can’t see you smiling. Are you smiling now? Roger, I saw your cup move, are you smiling right now? Honestly, I don’t know what’s going on around here, I think maybe I am sort of sick. You didn’t’ answer me, can I call in sick and spend the day with you?

(LONG PAUSE)

 

ROGER

In order, yes, no, yes, yes. How’s that? NOW WILL YOU SHUT UP?!?!?!? I’m trying to watch Carol Wayne and you’re just running off at the mouth. Yesyesyesyesyes!!

ELLEN FINISHES THE DISHES AND GOES TO THE PHONE.

ELLEN

Hello? Extension 233, please. Mr Beeler? No? Is this extension 233? Oh, I’m sorry. Yes, thank you. Bye.

ROGER GRABS THE PHONE

ROGER

Extension 233, dammit! Beeler? This is Roger Faltry, and my damned wife won’t be in to work today. No, the stupid thing got herself sick or something, and I can’t get her out of the damned bed! Yeah, Beeler, maybe tomorrow. Maybe tomorrow I’ll get a decent breakfast, too. Ha!

HE HANGS UP. ELLEN HAS BEEN ENJOYING THIS.

 

ELLEN

You were absolutely magnificent! Geez, Roger, you were just great! Don’t look so smug. Now, what to you want to do all day? My husband is so great and we’ll just do whatever he wants!

ROGER

Shut up, or I’ll call Beeler back and tell him I lied.

ELLEN

I’ll do anything, just please don’t sing.

ROGER

Anything?

ELLEN

Anything, Roger.

ROGER

Then keep your lid on, woman, so I can see Carol Wayne. I just told your TV boss you were sick so I could get you to shut up.

ELLEN

I don’t work at a TV station, Roger.

ROGER

I know that. TV is lousy. A TV boss is a lousy boss. TV is the furthest thing from art in the whole American economic system. A TV anything is a bad one. I know you don’t work for a TV station. I know everything. I just hate TV.

You watch it all the time. You’re just jealous because you can’t be on it.

ROGER

I’d be better than all those guys … I can sing, and brother, can I tap-dance! I could get on there with Carol Wayne and sing and tap-dance so good they’d start calling the Emmys Rogers.

ELLEN

They’d have to give one to the TV crew that could get you on camera, dummy. Aren’t you forgetting something? They’d gong you for lack of visible support! And what does Carol Wayne have that I ain’t got, anyway? Just look at how dumb she is…

ROGER

That’s just and act and you know it. What she’s got that you ain’t is a tan. And for putting her down, I’m gonna sing…

ELLEN

No, no, anything but that, please…
