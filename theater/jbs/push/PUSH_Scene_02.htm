SCENE 2: A Fancy Restaurant. Roger and Ellen are seated at a table with a candle in the middle and a waiter on the side.

ROGER

Ellen, you need to make up your mind. The waiter is waiting on you.

ELLEN

So what? He’s a waiter, isn’t he? Let the hungry jerk wait or we won’t give him a tip.

ROGER

Ellen!

ELLEN

Roger! I’m sorry, but I don’t get to go out like this very often and I want to be sure that I’ll remember what I got. I don’t wanna eat the same stuff I get at home, you know.

ROGER

You will be eating at home if you don’t make up your mind by the time I count to One. And you say that I’m picky about food.

 

ELLEN

Then I’ll just eat whatever you eat. Who knows, if I eat it once in a while I might even look thinner.

ROGER

Waiter, we’ll have the sirloin with a baked potato, medium, and Thousand Islands dressing. On the salad.

ELLEN

I want Roquefort.

ROGER

Roquefort for the lady. And we’ll drink wine, a red wine, choose it for us, if you will. We’ll have coffee after the meal.

WAITER

Sugar and cream?

ROGER

Two and one, please.

The waiter leaves.

ELLEN

I hate the way you say that, two and one. You just said it to spite me, didn’t you?

ROGER

No, you said you’d have whatever I had. You said something about losing weight, I think.

ELLEN

So you think I’m fat, do you? You’ve got a roll around your own tummy, you know. I felt it last week. You said you’ve been out jogging to stay in shape, but I’ll bet you’ve been out drinking the whole time.

ROGER

You know I never said you were fat. I don’t think that you’re fat. You’ve never been fat. You probably never will be fat. I wouldn’t car if you were fat. You can get as fat as you want, I don’t care if you get fat. I care that you talk all the time. I wish you’d lose some weight between your teeth.

ELLEN

Well, Roger, you never talk, and I never even know whether you’re in the room or gone to bed or what. I talk to get you to talk because I don’t know where you are. I wouldn’t talk as much if you just touched me once in a while or said something, anything, just to let me know you’re there. Is that asking too much, Roger? Just a little consideration?

ROGER

I’ve been thinking about talking and I think we have a major problem. I don’t think we’re compatible like we used to be. You used to be satisfied pretty easy, remember? Now, you don’t even notice it, you gripe all the time. You never open your mouth without demanding or whining or correcting and I worry about it. Where the hell is that TV waiter? I’m ready for my salad.

ELLEN

The waiter works on TV?

ROGER

If you could see my face right now, you wouldn’t be able to eat tonight. I know he doesn’t work at a TV station, I know everything. And you know considerably less. You’re really dumb sometimes, and I’m just totally confused as to why.

ELLEN

You don’t have to be so rude to me. I know I’m not as smart as you, Roger are, but I don’t hold that against you. I like you because you’re really funny when you’re happy. You’re just being mean to me because you’re so smart and you’re not doing anything. Well, you can just do it by yourself, because I’m leaving and I’ll see you at home. Take a taxi because I’m taking the car.

She exits.

ROGER

(Calling after her)

Ellen, please. You know how hard it is for me to get taxis … what about dinner? … well, you know ….

FADEOUT
