WHO'S HOT

Everyone is hot, as the Rangers are 11-4 in August, the best record in the majors for that period. When the don't pitch, they hit. They've jumped on Loiaza and Colon the past two nights, and are unconscious. They are hitting with men in scoring position, and they are beating down the walls. They are also fielding, throwing, and running the bases.

Colby Lewis had the first 10 strikeout game for Texas in years. Juan Dominguez completed a jump from A ball to the bigs, and looked sharp for 4 innings. Joaquin Benoit won 2 games in long relief by stopping the bleeding. Last night I realized that he is the spitting image of Kim Stone, the jazz bass player who is in the Rippingtons. And the Eraser? Well, he may just be in Arlington for good now.

WHO'S NOT

Diaz went 0 for 11 this week. He's not. Sadler went 0 for 7. He's not, either.

Ellis and Powell were battered around, they aren't hot either.

CLOSER LOOK: Is the Eraser here to stay?

Since his recall in July, Erasmo Ramirez has pitched 11 times. He gave up no runs in 9 of those outings. In 19.1 innings, he's allowed 2 runs, 9 hits, struck out 12, and walked 4. Not only that, but the last two game he was in, he went 3.0 and 2.2 innings - a lot more than a situational reliever. He made it all the way through the batting order.
