CLOSER LOOK: The New Bullpen

The Rangers bullpen this year was thought to be a real improvement. But this past week was a rough one for the bullpen. They pitched 19 innings, went 0-4, and had an ERA of 7.74. In a season when the team plays from behind, they really need the pen to hold the line so they can try to hit their way to a win. Bless their hearts, the offense has come from behind and battled a lot this year. But the pen is starting to slip.

In a typical season, a bullpen pitches about 500 innings. The Rangers bullpen has already pitched 234 innings, thru 59 games. They're headed for 700 innings. That extra 200 innings could get pretty ugly.

Besides the workload, it appears that Rangers management is cleaning house. Esteban Yan and Todd Van Poppel have already been released. Jay Powell can't be far behind. When Jay is getting the grounders, he's a good guy to have on the mound with the Rangers infield. Urbina is one of the names mentioned almost every time that the papers talk about trades. They say the Cardinals really need him.  

Ranger games might look very different at the end of the season, with Cordero and Fultz as the only survivors from the seven man pen that they had on opening day. Another member of the pen in April was the long forgotten CJ Nitkowski.
