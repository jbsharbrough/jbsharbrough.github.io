Choice Homes, Original Database, 1988.

Dale LeMaster was the Data Processing Manager at Oil States and gave me my first programming job in 1981. In 1988 he founded Choice Homes, a company with sales of over $400 million in 2001. My assignment in 1988 was to design a database to track the construction in process. I went to the construction superintendents and to Dale until I understood the requirements, and created the original application in interpretive dBase III. Later, we compiled it with Clipper, and then modified it for multiuser Novell. After that, it became an Access app, and now resides in SQL with an access front end. The basic data structures are unchanged.

Most interesting business challenge: Making it easy enough for superintendents to use. These are people who carry an amazing amount of information in their heads, and feel heavily time constrained. Many aren't comfortable as knowledge workers. By making walk lists for houses, we simplified the process of getting that information into a form that someone else could enter for them, and thereby keep the sales and managers informed about the status of production.

Most interesting technical challenge: Tracking revisions of bills of material. At first, there was only one subdivision. Later there were a hundred. A given floor plan was not manufactured the same way in any two of them - Choice is a very decentralized company. Keeping track of versions of bills of material was something that we did not handle well from the beginning, and that I would approach differently if I were to do it again.

Buzzwords: dBase, Clipper, Novell, MS Access.
